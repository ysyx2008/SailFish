## Why SailFish Terminal?

Ever had to—

- Juggle multiple servers and lose track of which window is which  
- Move files between your machine and remotes, one scp at a time  
- Want a solid terminal with a single UI and saved sessions  

SailFish Terminal does it: multi-tab terminal, SSH session management, and a dual-pane file manager. Local and remote side by side, drag-and-drop to transfer.

The **Steam version** gives you stable terminal and connection management with Steam’s auto-updates and easy install.  
For the **full version** (AI assistant, smart chat, local knowledge base, and more), get it free from the [official site](http://www.sfterm.com) or [GitHub](https://github.com/ysyx2008/SFTerminal).

---

## Multi-tab terminal: one window, many connections

- Local shell and multiple SSH hosts in one window; switch tabs to switch context  
- Sessions saved and grouped; pick up where you left off next time  
- Jump hosts, key/password auth—set it once and reuse  

---

## Dual-pane file manager: local ↔ remote, drag and drop

- Local on one side, remote on the other (or swap); browse both at a glance  
- Drag to upload or download—no need to remember scp/sftp commands  
- Create, rename, delete with a few clicks  

---

## SSH session management: simple and reliable

- Host list, groups, and notes in one place  
- Same experience on Windows, macOS, and Linux  

---

## More highlights

- **Cross-platform**: Windows / macOS / Linux (including Steam Deck)  
- **Themes**: Dark, light, and several color schemes  
- **Auto-updates**: Stay up to date through Steam

## Who is it for?

- **Ops & devs**: Manage servers and local terminals in one place; less fuss with files and sessions  
- **Students & beginners**: Clear UI and straightforward workflow to learn terminal and SSH  
- **Anyone who regularly connects to servers and moves files**: Less typing, more doing  

Whether you live in the terminal or are just getting started, SailFish Terminal makes remote and terminal work smoother.
